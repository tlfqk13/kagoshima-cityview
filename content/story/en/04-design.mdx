Once the features were decided, it was time to decide how it should look.

For the service to be adopted by Kagoshima City Hall's tourism division, it had to feel trustworthy. It couldn't look cheap. But it also couldn't be overly flashy.

I gathered references: paper maps from tourist information centers in Japan, traditional travel guidebooks from Kyoto, and the official Kagoshima tourism website.

I found a common language. **Serif fonts, cream backgrounds, restrained layouts.**

I defined the design tokens:
- Background: `#F4EFE9` — the color of aged paper
- Accent: `#8B4513` — the color of Sakurajima's volcanic soil
- Dark: `#1F1E1A` — the darkness of a night scene

The map pins speak the same language too: navy by default, brown for the selected bus stop, and warning orange for bus stops with incorrect Google Maps coordinates.

I made the design explain the features.
